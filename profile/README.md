## Overview

Managing the content and data on an iPhone or iPad from a Mac has become increasingly constrained as Apple has progressively restricted the file access and transfer capabilities of iTunes and Finder, prioritizing iCloud-based synchronization over direct device management. For users who need granular control over their device's content — transferring specific files, managing media libraries with precision, exporting message archives, or performing selective backup and restoration — the built-in macOS tools provide an incomplete and inflexible solution. <a href="#">iTools</a> provides the comprehensive device management capability that iTunes and Finder no longer offer, presenting a full-featured platform for accessing and managing all content categories on iPhone, iPad, and iPod directly from a Mac.

The file management capabilities in <a href="#">iTools</a> give users direct access to the content stored on their iOS devices without the restrictions that iTunes and iCloud impose. The music and media management module provides full two-way synchronization of music libraries with the ability to transfer individual tracks, albums, or playlists in either direction — from Mac to device or from device to Mac — without the one-way sync limitation that iTunes enforces. Photos and videos can be exported with their <a href="#">original quality</a> and album organization intact, and imported from the Mac with proper album assignment. The contacts and messages modules provide export capabilities in standard formats that make it straightforward to archive message histories, migrate contacts to new devices or services, and maintain backups of personal communications that are not covered by standard iCloud backup configurations.

The application management tools in <a href="#">iTools</a> extend the application's utility to iOS app workflows that iTunes no longer supports. Apps can be installed from IPA files, and the <a href="#">app sandbox browser</a> provides access to the document storage within individual application containers, allowing files created or stored by specific apps to be transferred to the Mac for backup, editing, or migration. The device information panel provides detailed hardware and software specifications including serial number, hardware identifiers, storage breakdown by content type, and iOS version details that are useful for support, inventory, and troubleshooting purposes. <a href="#">iTools OSX</a> restores the comprehensive device management capabilities that professional iOS users expect.
